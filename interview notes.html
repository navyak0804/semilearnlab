Interview Notes


Interview Questions:


  1. What would you say the difficulty of your AP Science class is on a scale of 1-10 and why?

  2. How do you find quality resources for your AP science classes?

  3. What do you classify as a quality resource to study?

  4. What type of resources keep you engaged in learning?

  5. Are you aware of your learning style? If so, what learning style suits you best? (visual, auditory, read/write, kinesthetic)

  6. Tell me about some of the pros and cons of your teacher’s teaching style.

  7. How often do you study for each AP science class? Does each learning session last for a long or short time? If long, why do you feel like it takes you that much time?

  8. Does finding resources take part of your studying time?

  9. Name some of the resources that you use to study for (AP science) class. What are the pros and cons of those resources that you’ve experienced?

  10. How motivated are you to learn and study for your AP science classes? Based on your response, why are you motivated/not motivated to study?

  11. Did you use any AP-specific prep materials (Princeton Review, Barron’s)? How helpful were they to other resources? What is your favorite prep material?

  12. Do you think these AP prep materials are good for studying or just for review?

  13. How do you feel about a streak system, like Duolingo, to keep you motivated to study?

  14. What additional resources do you wish existed for AP science classes that you haven’t found?

  15. What are some things that you look for in a studying platform/database?


Interview Feeback:


  • Quality resources are characterized by the inclusion of questions, answers, and detailed explanations. Such materials engage students by providing comprehensive practice and clear explanations all in one place.

  • All interviewed students were aware of their learning styles, with the majority identifying as read/write and visual learners.

  • Many students requested quizzes that not only provide the correct answers but also explain why the other options are incorrect.

  • Some students reported that sourcing study materials does not take much time as they rely on teacher-provided resources. However, those who seek additional materials spend considerable time finding the most helpful resources.

  • Study time for AP Science classes varies, typically ranging from 30 minutes to 1 hour daily or 3 to 5 hours before major tests.

  • At the beginning of the year, students have not extensively used AP prep materials like Barron's or Princeton Review, although they find these resources useful for review rather than initial learning.

  • Students are generally motivated to study for their AP Science classes, driven by a desire to perform well irrespective of the difficulty of the content.
